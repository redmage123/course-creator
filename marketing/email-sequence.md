Quick one today — I want to make sure you know about labs.

If you're building technical training — anything involving software, tools, command-line work, cloud platforms — you can attach a real lab environment to your course. Not a simulation. Not a video. A live Docker container that spins up in your learner's browser.

Your learner opens the lesson, clicks "Launch Lab," and gets a working environment: terminal, files, a running application — whatever the lab requires. When they're done, it's cleaned up automatically. No setup on their end. No IT ticket required.

Here's where this matters:

- **DevOps training:** Real Kubernetes clusters. Terraform environments. CI/CD pipelines.

## Email 4 — Invite Your Team / Students

**Subject A:** TechUni works better with your whole team
**Subject B:** Ready to bring people in?
**Preview:** Enroll your team in under 5 minutes →
**Send timing:** Day 9

---

Hi {{first_name}},

You've built a course. Now it's time to use it.

Enrolling your team in TechUni takes about five minutes. Here's how:

**Option 1: Direct invite**
Go to Organization → Members → Invite. Paste in email addresses (one per line or comma-separated). They'll get an email with a direct link to your course.

**Option 2: Enrollment link**
Go to your course → Settings → Share Link. Copy the enrollment URL and paste it into Slack, Teams, or your onboarding doc. Anyone who clicks it gets instant access.

**Option 3: CSV import**
For larger teams: Organization → Members → Import CSV. Works for 5 or 500 learners.

Once people are enrolled, you'll see their progress in real time: which lessons they've completed, how they scored on assessments, and where they're getting stuck.

Most managers tell us the analytics are the part they didn't expect to care about — until they saw a learner stuck on the same lesson three times.
